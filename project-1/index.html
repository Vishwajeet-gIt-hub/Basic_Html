Abdul Kalam, was a renowned Indian scientist, teacher, author, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family, Kalam’s journey from selling newspapers as a child to becoming one of the most respected and loved leaders of India is truly inspirational. Despite financial struggles, his thirst for knowledge and deep interest in science and technology shaped his remarkable life. Dr. Kalam studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. After completing his studies, he joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a crucial role in India’s space and missile development programs. He was instrumental in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980. His contributions earned him the title “Missile Man of India” for his work on the development of ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi.
